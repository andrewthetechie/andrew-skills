# Market Sizing

Use this reference for TAM/SAM/SOM, market growth, opportunity validation, and segment prioritization.

## Define the market first

Specify the customer, problem or use case, product category, geography, channel, and time period. Define TAM, SAM, and SOM for this analysis instead of assuming the labels have universal meanings.

- **TAM:** total annual revenue opportunity under the stated market boundary.
- **SAM:** portion the current product and business model can serve.
- **SOM:** plausible near-term share under explicit capacity and adoption constraints.

## Choose methods

Use at least one primary method and, for consequential estimates, one independent cross-check.

### Bottom-up

Prefer when customer counts and pricing are knowable.

`annual opportunity = eligible customers × annual revenue per customer`

For usage models, decompose annual revenue per customer into units, frequency, price, and take rate. Keep segment overlap and multi-location customers explicit.

### Top-down

Use as a cross-check when a credible market total exists.

`addressable opportunity = market total × geography share × segment share × product-fit share`

Explain why every filter applies. Do not multiply unrelated percentages or present an analyst headline as a directly addressable market.

### Value theory

Use for a new category with weak spending data.

`opportunity = target customers × economic value created × defensible value-capture rate`

Support value created with a workflow baseline, such as time saved, loss avoided, or revenue gained.

## Build the estimate

1. Select mutually exclusive customer segments.
2. Record every input with unit, year, geography, and source or assumption label.
3. Calculate low, base, and high cases from meaningful drivers rather than arbitrary percentage bands.
4. Derive SAM from real product, regulatory, language, geography, and channel constraints.
5. Derive SOM from sales capacity, conversion, adoption timing, retention, and competition—not a blanket market-share percentage.
6. Cross-check against comparable company revenue, observed budgets, or another sizing method.

## Report

Show the market definition, method, assumption table, TAM/SAM/SOM range, top sensitivities, cross-check, exclusions, and implications. Use one currency and period throughout.

Example: 12,000 eligible sites × $6,000 annual contract value = $72 million bottom-up TAM. If the product can initially serve 40% of sites, SAM is $28.8 million before adoption or sales-capacity constraints are applied to SOM.
