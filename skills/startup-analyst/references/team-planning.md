# Team Planning

Use this reference for hiring sequence, organizational capacity, role design, compensation analysis, and contractor-versus-employee choices.

## Plan from milestones

1. State the company stage, current team, funded runway, and next value-creating milestone.
2. Translate the milestone into required outcomes and recurring work.
3. Identify the constraint: missing capability, insufficient capacity, unclear ownership, or poor process.
4. Consider founder ownership, automation, a contractor, an agency, a fractional leader, or a full-time hire.
5. Define each role by 6- and 12-month outcomes, not a title alone.
6. Sequence hires by dependency, recruiting lead time, cash impact, and milestone criticality.
7. Add hiring dates and fully loaded costs to the financial model.

`fully loaded cost = cash compensation + payroll burden + benefits + recruiting + equipment + allocated overhead`

For revenue roles, model ramp time, quota capacity, attainment, and manager span. For product and engineering roles, connect capacity assumptions to a specific roadmap or reliability outcome.

## Compensation and equity

Current compensation, employment classification, and equity practices vary by geography, stage, role, and date. Research comparable, current sources; label the vintage and percentile. Treat tax, securities, and employment matters as issues for qualified advisers.

## Output

Provide the capability gap, alternatives considered, recommended role and outcomes, hire trigger, sequence and dependencies, loaded cash cost, runway impact, recruiting risks, and a fallback if hiring is delayed.

Reject prestige hiring, executive titles without executive scope, unsupported span-of-control rules, and headcount plans that are disconnected from cash and milestones.
