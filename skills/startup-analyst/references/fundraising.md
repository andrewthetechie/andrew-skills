# Fundraising

Use this reference for round sizing, timing, dilution scenarios, milestone planning, investor readiness, and use-of-funds analysis.

## Start with the financing objective

Define the milestone the round must fund and why reaching it should improve company value or financing options. Work backward through time, resources, cash needs, fundraising lead time, and contingency.

## Round-size workflow

1. Build the monthly operating plan through the milestone.
2. Include hiring ramp, working capital, one-time costs, debt obligations, and realistic fundraising timing.
3. Calculate the peak cumulative cash deficit and required minimum cash balance.
4. Add a clearly justified contingency; do not hide it inside inflated operating assumptions.
5. Compare the resulting raise with execution capacity and plausible dilution constraints.
6. Model delayed growth, delayed fundraising, and a smaller round.

Useful formulas for a priced round:

- `post-money valuation = pre-money valuation + new investment`
- `new-investor ownership = new investment / post-money valuation`
- `existing-holder dilution = 1 - pre-money valuation / post-money valuation`

Model option-pool changes, SAFEs, notes, discounts, caps, and pro rata rights explicitly when relevant; their order and legal terms matter. Label cap-table outputs as scenarios and direct legal or tax interpretation to qualified counsel.

## Readiness test

Check whether the company can explain, with internally consistent evidence:

- customer problem, product, and timing;
- market definition and wedge;
- traction, retention, and unit economics appropriate to stage;
- competitive alternatives and defensibility;
- operating plan, use of funds, and milestone unlocked;
- risks, open questions, and why this team can execute.

## Output

Provide the recommended range, runway and milestone logic, monthly use of funds, dilution or instrument scenarios, assumptions, cash-out dates, downside plan, fundraising start trigger, and unresolved legal or financial questions.

Do not reverse-engineer a raise from a desired valuation, promise investor outcomes, or cite “typical” terms without current stage-, geography-, and instrument-specific evidence.
