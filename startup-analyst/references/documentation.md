# Decision Documents

Use this reference when producing a memo, business case, board update, investor analysis, model specification, or other durable artifact.

## Choose the artifact

### Decision memo

1. Decision and recommendation
2. Context and constraints
3. Options and decision criteria
4. Evidence and analysis
5. Risks, sensitivities, and dissenting case
6. Actions, owners, dates, and revisit triggers

### Analytical report or business case

1. Executive summary
2. Scope, definitions, and methodology
3. Inputs and assumption register
4. Findings and calculations
5. Scenarios and validation
6. Recommendation and implementation implications
7. Sources, limitations, and appendix

### Board or investor update

1. What changed and why it matters
2. Performance against plan with consistent metric definitions
3. Cash, runway, forecast, and material variances
4. Wins, misses, risks, and lessons
5. Priorities, decisions, and specific asks

## Consistency checks

- Use one as-of date, currency, scenario vocabulary, and metric dictionary.
- Reconcile headline figures to tables and appendices.
- Keep facts, assumptions, estimates, and recommendations visibly distinct.
- Put the decision and material caveats in the main document, not only an appendix.
- Cite external facts at the point of use and preserve source dates.
- Remove empty headings, placeholder language, unsupported superlatives, and decorative framework content.

Write for the named audience. A founder operating memo should optimize for action; a board document should highlight material changes and decisions; an investor artifact should be concise, internally consistent, and diligence-ready.
